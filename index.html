The capital of the world keeps getting better

The density and the diversity. New York City is, by far, the densest city in the United States- and that includes the outer boroughs. Manhattan alone is one of the densest urban areas in the world, especially in the developed world. (The only comparable place I've been in the so-called 'first world' is Hong Kong, which is a whole other story).

People think of Manhattan as a rich person's territory- justifiably- but bear in mind that there's Harlem, and Washington Heights, and Morningside Heights, as well as a multitude of areas with a lot of working-class and middle-class people. Because of this geographic compression and a flat-rate subway, New York's various populations mix and mingle more than those in many other cities.

Views for an entire lifetime

Guided tours, from helicopters to the top of some of the world's tallest buildings, you'll always find a way to fill your mind and eyes with the history of the world's most famous city. All kinds of people can be found in it and it offers transportation by air, water, and land depending on your needs.

Important museums and preserved buildings can be found all over the city, it never stops moving or growing and there is always a corner. The city itself is a historical masterpiece, conserving the new among the old and stating its power by letting you see that this city cannot be less than the mere meaning of progress.

History has struck it with millions of different lesson and leading minds who have all contributed to it, every corner has a bit of every century. Every building offers you a piece of history and transports you to the very moment everything happened, from cathedrals to landscapes from 800 years ago, the city knows how to mix modern with classic.
